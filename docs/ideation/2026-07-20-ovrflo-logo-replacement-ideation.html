Grounding Context

Codebase context. Design system "Architectural Dark" (DESIGN.md): obsidian #050505 base, chalk #f4f4f4, 1px graphite borders on a 40px grid, accent-cyan = borrow side and accent-gold = lend side ("strictly semantic, never decorative"), Inter + IBM Plex Mono, sharp corners (max 2px), bans on gradients/shadows/blur/bubbly shapes, ASCII diagrams as a first-class element. Current mark: organic blue gradient wave-circle — violates the gradient ban and the palette, and its edge-touching detail fights the maskable-icon 80% safe zone. Surfaces: 28px header (beside the mono wordmark), 16/32px favicons + 180px apple-touch icon, OG image (currently a 292×290 JPEG — wrong ratio for 1200×630 OG), future app header (currently text-only). Landing stats vocabulary: None / Streamed / Fixed.

Past learnings. The discarded Next app generated its favicon as a plain geometric "O" (web/app/icon.tsx, pre-dating the current palette) — weak but real precedent for a letterform favicon. No brand decisions are documented in docs/solutions/; whatever wins here should be captured. A stale pre-DESIGN.md plan (plans/ovfl-frontend-redesign-liquid-flow-gold.md) still contains superseded liquid/gradient brand language — flagged non-authoritative.

External research (moderate value): Morpho = durable abstract symbol; Pendle = line-based motion mark; Alchemix (closest product analog) has weak brand presence; no DeFi protocol has a terminal/ASCII-native brand in production — an open differentiation gap. 2026 trends: abstract-geometric vs monogram resurgence; black/white minimalism resurgent; metallic/chrome an anti-pattern for this system. Loop motifs are the established signal for "self-sustaining process." Favicon practice: Safari ignores SVG-favicon media queries (ICO fallback required); maskable icons need an 80% safe zone.

Evidence dossiers (verbatim quotes with file:line): /tmp/compound-engineering-501/ce-ideate/l0g0a1b2/evidence-design-language.md (47 entries) and …/evidence-brand-language.md (27 quotes + 4 ASCII diagrams). Fleet: 5 ideation agents (3 mid-tier, 2 top-tier), 48 raw candidates, fresh-context basis verification on all 30 merged directions.

RANK 1 — TOP PICK

Terminal-Native Identity System

axis: geometry-style + application-surfacesconfidence 80%complexity Medium

The identity is built from the protocol's own documentation grammar: the canonical mark is a ≤5-character-wide, 3-line box-drawing tile whose master lives as text in the README, versioned in git like the contracts. SVG mark and favicons are mechanical vectorizations of the tile on a strict character grid; the wordmark stays pure IBM Plex Mono (optionally with a terminal block cursor); the OG/social image becomes the OVRFLO CYCLE ASCII diagram itself with the wordmark as a corner label — so a pasted link shows the machine, not a badge.

illustrative — tile glyphs, favicon crop, and cursor lockup are directional, not final art
basis — direct: DESIGN.md:45 "ASCII diagrams are a first-class element: monospace, chalk on obsidian… hand-aligned"; the four verbatim box-drawing diagrams in README.md; app-tables-v2.html:33 header is already text-only. external: research found no terminal/ASCII-native DeFi brand in production. Verifier: sound (5 candidates from 5 different frames converged here).

rationaleThe brand's densest existing visual output is its diagrams — this makes the logo speak the house language instead of being the one off-system asset. Claims a verified category gap at near-zero production cost: the mark renders in a git diff, a code comment, an Etherscan tag, and a tweet without an asset pipeline. Fixes the wrong-ratio OG image as a side effect.

downsidesReads "developer-first" — if the target audience shifts retail, a glyph tile may feel austere. Box-drawing glyph rendering varies across fonts outside controlled surfaces (mitigated: canonical renderings are vectorized, only the master is text). Choosing the exact tile is a real design exercise.

RANK 2

Carry-Bit Ring (Integer-Overflow O)

axis: mark-conceptconfidence 75%complexity Low

A single-stroke "O" that overshoots its own start point and steps outward with one hard 90° jog — a register wrapping past its maximum, the carry bit escaping. Roughly 1.1 turns of a circle plus a short rectilinear tail; monochrome chalk (tail optionally gold only in semantically-gold contexts). One SVG path serves 16px (broken ring with tail) through 180px (the 90° step resolves). Synthesis S1: the tail's length can double as a vesting-progress indicator in app contexts.

illustrative — stroke geometry directional; the jog would sit on the 40px grid module
basis — direct: CONCEPTS.md:107-109 — the fixed discount "overflows into extractable value" (verified verbatim). external: integer overflow / odometer rollover; the audience (Solidity devs) reads "overflow" as the carry bit before water. reasoned: every loop-logo in the category is a closed loop — an open loop with a carry-out tail encodes overflow rather than mere circulation. Verifier: sound.

rationaleName, mechanism, and audience in-joke collapse into one stroke. A monogram (it is still an O) with a story no competitor can reuse, fully compliant with the sharp-corner/monochrome rules, and trivially maskable (tail stays inside the 80% safe zone).

downsidesThe in-joke is invisible to non-developers — it reads as "broken circle with a tab" without the story. The 90° jog needs careful drawing to avoid reading as a "Q" or a power symbol.

RANK 3

Breached-Letterform Ō (Collapsed Lockup)

axis: mark-concept + application-surfacesconfidence 70%complexity Low

A typographic mark: the mono "O" with a horizontal limit-line floating above it — a max-capacity line — where the O's top arc visibly breaks through the line. Two elements total. Deployed as the first letter of the OVRFLO wordmark (one lockup file; the favicon is a crop of that O), which finally makes the landing header and the already-text-only app header the same system.

illustrative — breach depth and line weights directional
basis — direct: AGENTS.md:104 naming rule (the compressed name OVRFLO already drops letters — a mutated letterform continues that logic); styles.css:67-73 `.logo` is already pure mono type, so the lockup drops in with zero redesign (both verified). reasoned: single letterforms are the pictorial device that best survives 16px. Weak-but-real continuity: the discarded app's generated favicon was a plain "O" (web/app/icon.tsx, per learnings — arbitration note: the verifier checked only the mockup assets and missed this).

rationaleCheapest credible path to a real mark: no separate pictorial asset to maintain, one drawing serves every surface, and it depicts the name's meaning (a thing exceeding its limit) without water.

downsidesSubtle at a glance — risks reading as a stylistic quirk rather than a mark. The macron/limit-line needs enough breach to survive 16px rasterization.

RANK 4

Overflowing Card

axis: geometry-styleconfidence 65%complexity Low

A 1px chalk-stroked sharp-cornered square — identical grammar to the design system's cards — with a single horizontal stroke that begins inside and continues through the right border, terminating outside: overflow: visible rendered as a mark. Monochrome only (the verifier's compliance pass removed the "optionally gold" hedge). At 16px it's a box with a protruding tick — cursor-like in a tab bar.

illustrative — proportions directional
basis — direct: DESIGN.md:30 card rule ("No border-radius… Sharp edges… 1px graphite borders") and README.md:300-315 "What's fixed will overflow" (both verified). external: the CSS-overflow / buffer-overflow reading is the developer audience's native decoding of the word. Verifier: weak→sound once resolved monochrome.

rationaleThe only concept built from the design system's own components — the logo is literally one of the product's cards breaking the rule that contains it. Zero risk of palette or geometry mismatch by construction.

downsidesBox-plus-line is a crowded genre (risks reading as an "export" or "logout" icon); the escaping-stroke length is doing all the distinctive work.

RANK 5

Live Vesting Favicon

axis: application-surfacesconfidence 75%complexity Medium

A surface strategy composable with any mark above: the favicon doesn't show the logo — it shows state. A 16×16 canvas favicon: 1px chalk frame, interior filling bottom-up in gold proportional to the connected user's vested percentage (protocol aggregate when signed out). Static ICO fallback (Safari) frozen at a canonical fill. The browser tab becomes the product demo: a loan repaying itself while you watch.

illustrative — the tab icon as a progress bar of your own money vesting
basis — direct: DESIGN.md:69 "The only permitted 'live' motion is data itself updating (e.g. a stream's vested amount ticking)" — the verifier called this "a near-perfect, precise match… genuinely rare"; the ICO/PNG fallback chain already exists in the landing head (verified). Gold = vested yield is a legitimate semantic use, not decoration.

rationaleTurns the smallest brand surface into the product's differentiator demonstrated live. No competitor's tab does anything. Composable: adopt with idea 1, 2, 3, 4, or 6.

downsidesNeeds the app (P3+) and a canvas-favicon runtime; some browsers throttle favicon updates in background tabs; must degrade gracefully signed-out.

RANK 6

Open-Circuit Loop

axis: mark-conceptconfidence 60%complexity Low

One unbroken 1px polyline, right angles only, drawn like a PCB trace: it enters horizontally (deposit), turns through a rectangular near-loop (the self-repaying cycle), and exits without closing — because "You are never locked in." The refusal to close is the point: it takes the category's established loop signal and breaks the cliché exactly where OVRFLO differs from locked protocols.

illustrative — routing directional; would align to the 40px grid
basis — direct: landing-v3.html:102-106 "Leave whenever you want… You are never locked in" (verified verbatim). external: loop motif = established "self-sustaining" signal; closest analog (Alchemix) holds the territory weakly. Verifier: sound.

rationaleEncodes both halves of the pitch — self-repaying (the loop) and exit-optionality (the opening) — in one stroke, in house geometry.

downsidesAbstract polylines are the least ownable genre in the set (adjacent to circuit-board clichés); at 16px the routing must simplify to survive.

RANK 7 — ADOPT REGARDLESS OF GEOMETRY

Mark Production Policy (Pipeline + currentColor + Two-Tier)

axis: palette-fit + scale-legibility + application-surfacesconfidence 90%complexity Low

Three orthogonal policies that apply to whichever mark wins. (1) Single-source pipeline: one SVG master (or token-driven generator) emits the full favicon/ICO/PNG/apple-touch/OG set at build time, with the maskable 80% safe zone enforced by the generator — replacing the six hand-exported raster files that can silently disagree today. (2) currentColor rule: the mark is defined colorless (fill: currentColor) — chalk in neutral chrome, inheriting cyan/gold only when standing in a semantically colored context; it can never drift off-palette because it has no palette. (3) Two-tier reduction: a detailed ≥64px rendition and a deliberately reduced 16–32px kernel from one construction grid, instead of one drawing squinted down six sizes.

basis — direct: six separately-produced raster files at inconsistent bit depths in mockups/images/ (verifier-corrected count); DESIGN.md:35 semantic-accent rule; the header's chalk-only convention. external: Safari SVG-favicon gap and maskable safe-zone requirements are generator-enforceable, not designer-memorable. Verifier: sound (called the currentColor rule "implementation hygiene… should accompany whichever mark is chosen").

rationaleThe white-box saga and the off-palette drift were process failures, not shape failures — no geometry fixes them. These policies make the next mark unable to regress, whatever it looks like.

downsidesSmall build-step addition to the web rebuild (fits P2's typegen/build tooling naturally).
